## Insurance Categories

### 🏥 Health Insurance

Health insurance covers the cost of medical care — from routine check-ups to major surgeries — so that unexpected illness or injury doesn't become a financial crisis.

#### What It Typically Covers
- Doctor and specialist visits
- Hospitalization and surgery
- Emergency care
- Prescription medications
- Diagnostic tests (lab work, imaging)
- Preventive care (vaccinations, screenings)
- Mental health and therapy
- Maternity and newborn care (on many plans)

#### Key Concepts to Know
| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Premium** | The monthly amount you pay for coverage |
| **Deductible** | Amount you pay out-of-pocket before insurance kicks in |
| **Co-pay** | Fixed fee you pay per visit or prescription |
| **Co-insurance** | Your share (%) of costs after meeting the deductible |
| **Out-of-pocket maximum** | The most you'll ever pay in a year — the insurer covers the rest |
| **Network** | Doctors and hospitals contracted with your insurer |
| **Pre-existing condition** | A health issue you had before getting insured |

#### Types of Health Plans
- **Individual Plan** — Covers one person
- **Family Plan** — Covers you, your spouse, and dependent children
- **HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)** — Lower cost, requires a primary care physician and referrals
- **PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)** — More flexibility to see specialists without referrals
- **High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)** — Lower premiums, higher deductible; often paired with a savings account

#### Who Should Consider It
Everyone. Health insurance is one of the most essential forms of protection — unexpected medical events can cost tens of thousands without it.

### 🛡️ Life Insurance

Life insurance pays a tax-free lump sum (the "death benefit") to your designated beneficiaries when you pass away. It provides financial security for the people who depend on you.

#### What It Covers
- Income replacement for your family
- Mortgage and debt repayment
- Children's education costs
- Funeral and final expenses
- Business continuity (for business owners)

#### Key Concepts to Know
| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Death Benefit** | The amount paid to beneficiaries upon your death |
| **Beneficiary** | The person(s) who receive the payout |
| **Premium** | Regular payments to keep the policy active |
| **Term** | The duration of coverage (for term policies) |
| **Cash Value** | Savings component that builds over time (permanent policies only) |
| **Rider** | Optional add-ons to customize your policy |

#### Types of Life Insurance
- **Term Life Insurance** — Covers a fixed period (10, 20, or 30 years). Affordable, straightforward — pays out only if you die during the term. Ideal for income replacement during working years.
- **Whole Life Insurance** — Lifelong coverage with a guaranteed death benefit and a savings component (cash value) that grows over time.
- **Universal Life Insurance** — Flexible lifelong coverage; allows you to adjust premiums and death benefit as your life changes.
- **Variable Life Insurance** — Permanent coverage with an investment component; cash value tied to market performance.

#### Who Should Consider It
Anyone with dependents — a spouse, children, aging parents — or significant financial obligations like a mortgage or business debt. The younger and healthier you are when you buy, the lower your premium.

### 🚗 Auto Insurance

Auto insurance protects you financially in case of accidents, theft, or damage involving your vehicle. In most places, a minimum level of coverage is legally required to drive.

#### What It Typically Covers
- Damage to other people's vehicles or property (liability)
- Injuries to other people caused by your driving
- Damage to your own vehicle from accidents
- Theft, vandalism, fire, and natural disasters
- Medical costs for you and your passengers
- Uninsured/underinsured driver incidents

--- 

#### Key Concepts to Know
| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Premium** | The amount you pay (monthly or annually) for coverage |
| **Deductible** | What you pay first before the insurer covers a claim |
| **Liability Limit** | Maximum amount the insurer will pay per incident |
| **No-Claims Bonus** | Discount earned for every claim-free year |
| **Excess** | Another term for deductible used in some regions |
| **Comprehensive vs. Third-Party** | Comprehensive covers your car too; third-party only covers others |

#### Types of Auto Coverage
- **Third-Party Liability** — Minimum legal requirement; covers damage/injury to *others* only
- **Third-Party, Fire & Theft** — Adds protection against fire and vehicle theft
- **Comprehensive Coverage** — The broadest protection; covers your car and others regardless of fault
- **Collision Coverage** — Covers repair/replacement of your car after a collision
- **Personal Injury Protection (PIP)** — Covers medical expenses for you and passengers
- **Uninsured Motorist Coverage** — Protects you if the at-fault driver has no insurance
- **Roadside Assistance** — Add-on covering towing, flat tires, and emergency services

#### Factors That Affect Your Premium
- Age and driving experience
- Driving record and claims history
- Vehicle make, model, and age
- Annual mileage
- Location (urban vs. rural)
- Chosen coverage level and deductible

#### Who Should Consider It
Anyone who owns or regularly drives a vehicle. Comprehensive coverage is highly recommended — the cost of repairs or liability after an accident can far exceed the annual premium.
